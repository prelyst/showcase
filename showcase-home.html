Questions

Things people
actually ask.

Do you read my existing content from other platforms?
No. Showcase only publishes — we don't ingest or scrape. Every post on Showcase exists because you wrote it here and chose to also send it elsewhere. Your archive grows as you publish. This is a deliberate decision: it keeps us on the right side of every platform's terms of service and means you always know exactly what's going out.
What happens when a platform's API changes or breaks?
Each platform has an independent "lane" with its own state machine. If X has an outage, your LinkedIn post still goes. If TikTok's API changes, only TikTok pauses. We monitor per-platform success rates and ship fixes fast — transparency on our status page is part of the deal.
Can I edit a post after it's been cross-posted?
You can edit the Showcase version. Cross-posted copies on other platforms stay as they were published — different platforms handle edits differently, and pushing edits can sometimes trigger algorithmic downranking. For now we show you the tradeoff and let you decide whether to manually edit the copies that matter.
Why isn't Instagram available yet?
Instagram and TikTok both require app-review approval from Meta and ByteDance respectively before a third-party can publish on users' behalf. Our submissions are in review. The moment either one clears, existing Creator tier subscribers get access first. Heads up: Instagram will only work with Business or Creator accounts — Meta locks personal accounts out of programmatic publishing.
Is my Showcase profile public?
Yes by default, because that's the portfolio value. You can set individual posts to private (visible only to you) or hidden from your profile (still exists on the other platforms but not listed on Showcase). Full account privacy — only visible to followers — is on the roadmap.
Why should I pay for this instead of using Buffer?
Buffer is a scheduling tool built for marketing teams. Showcase is a publishing platform built for creators — it gives you a profile, a feed, and a native audience in addition to cross-posting. The cross-posting itself is competitive on price ($8 vs Buffer's $6/channel which adds up fast). If you only want to queue tweets and you don't care about a portfolio, Buffer's great. If you want one URL that represents everything you publish, you want Showcase.
Do you train AI on my posts?
No. Your posts are used to render your profile, serve the feed to your followers, and publish to the platforms you select. That's it. No model training, no data sales, no ads sold against your content. If that ever changes we'd tell you clearly and let you opt out before it happened.
